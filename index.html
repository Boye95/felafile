FELA ANIKULAPO KUTI

“He who carried death in his pouch”

FACTS ABOUT FELA YOU SHOULD KNOW

  1. birth—Fela was born—Olufela Olusegun Oludotun Ransome-Kuti on 15 October 1938 in Abeokuta, Ogun State.
  2. the Beginning—He was sent to London in 1958 to study medicine but decided to study music at the Trinity College of Music, the trumoet was his preferred instrument. While there, he formed the band Koola Lobitos(his first band), playing a fusion of jazz and highlife.
  3. afrobeat—He first called his music Afrobeat, a combination of highlife, funk, jazz, salsa, calypso and traditional Yoruba music.
  4. influence—He was influenced politically during his stay in the United States where he discovered the Black Power movement through Sandra Smith(Izsadore).
  5. kalakuta—He formed the Kalakuta Republic—a commune, a recording studio, and a home—for the many people connected to the band that he later declared independent from the Nigerian state.
  6. anikulapo—He changed his name to Anikulapo (meaning "He who carries death in his pouch", with the interpretation: "I will be the master of my own destiny and will decide when it is time for death to take me"). He stopped using the hyphenated surname "Ransome" because it was a slave name.
  7. activism—Kuti was highly engaged in political activism in Africa from the 1970s until his death. He criticized the corruption of Nigerian Government officials and the mistreatment of Nigerian citizens.
  8. legacy—Kuti is remembered as an influential icon who was brave enough to boldly voice his opinions on matters that affected the nation through his music. An annual festival, "Felabration," is held each year to celebrate the life of this music legend and his birthday.
